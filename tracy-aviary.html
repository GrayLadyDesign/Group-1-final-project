Tracy Aviary

Project Description

As a group project during the 2022 UX/UI bootcamp, I was part of a team that redesigned the payment, donation, and membership flows for Tracy Aviary, a bird Sanctuary located in Salt Lake City, Utah. Though this project was for practice, I gained much insight from applying the design process in a real world scenario.

My Role:

UX/UI Designer


Tools used:

Miro, Figma, Google, Adobe Creative Cloud, Trello

Project Overview



The Problem

Tracy Aviary is struggling to get repeat customers to visit the sanctuary, and few people purchase memberships or offer yearly donations. Continued lack of donation and low membership sales may leave Tracy Aviary unsustainable.

The Goal

Our focus became the user path for both donations and daily admissions. In addition, we wanted to emphasize across the site that yearly memberships come with great benefits and should be considered.

Research



At first glance, the site looks properly taken care of
    • Obvious recent UX/UI redesign
    • Archetypal elements consistent across each web page
    • Showcasing of the birds with gorgeous imagery

    As I dug a little deeper, I noticed:
    • Link to admissions page was regularly disabled
    • There was much confusion surrounding membership benefits
    • Layout shifted drastically for each check out page for membership, donation, and admission pages

    I wanted to know if users of this site were experience similiar mishaps, so I turned to my trusty side-kick, Google Forms and declared "It's survey time!"

User Interviews



The findings were simple yet informative, confirming our hypothesis was correct.
    • The ability to purchase admissions online was very hit-or-miss depending on the day or time of day
    • Donation page is overwhelming and a grid wasn’t used, making for a confusing checkout procedure
    • Users were dropping off and not completing a donations or admissions
    • Those lovely eyecatching images and bright colors are lovely, but also prove very overwhelming during navigation

Style Guide



A few new guidelines were laid out for the UI of the site.
    • Making the buttons more legible by replacing the textured background with a solid darker teal that passes AA
    • Iconography that was consistent on every page - including the checkout pages
    • Font style & Typography that was consistent on every page - inlcuding the checkout pages
    • Chromatic harmony follows rules - a mature color pallet reflected as more muted greens and yellows and the addition of a light grounding earth tone

    Styletiles are one of the greatest joys this UX life has to offer.

Usability Testing



We ran usability testing on 7+ users on two different mobile versions as well as two different web high fidelity versions for side by side comparison. A summary of our key findings includes:
    • Fixed header vs Stationary Header
    • Side navigation vs No side navigation
    • Replaced the side nav bar with the fixed header to reduce redundency on the site/mobile app. We noticed our users were experience option overwhelm.
    • Added a link to Admissions page from the Admissions popup from the Homepage

Final Prototype



I, along with my team, had achieved the UX adjustments we set out to accomplish!
    • Updated navigation for the donations Page and implemented the use of a grid for uniformity on all pages
    • Simplified the flow for admissions - visual continuity to the rest of the site
    • Added a membership prompt during checkout to bring awareness to the community and boost sales
    • Created a breadcrumb for web and a fixed header for web/mobile versions
    Final Prototype

In Summary



Though I achieved the UX adjustments I set out to accomplish with the short time span I was alloted for this exercise, I recognize this project is far from complete. Here are a few items I would like to still add to my redesign of the Tracy Aviary website:
    • The ability to purchase admissions online was very hit-or-miss depending on the day or time of day
    • Grid out and integrate the shop section so it does not open in a separate tab/site
    • Animated icons in the shop section
    • Aviary themed animations and interactions throughout the site
    • Prototyped full fidelity flow for tablet and iPad
